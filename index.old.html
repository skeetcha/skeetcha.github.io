About Me

Ever since I can remember, my passion has been to create things. I remember when I was a kid that I had written a play about a cloud, the sun, the wind, and the sky (or something to that effect, it was long ago). As I've grown up, I've gained skills to give me even more outlets for my abundant creativity (because I definitely need more stuff to do!).

Nowadays, I program and run tests on graphics cards for a living, while on the side, I write my own songs and arrange music for wind ensemble, orchestra, vocal acapella, and jazz band. Recently, I've also taken to writing fiction when I'm not creating languages or worlds for people to read about.

Meme Machine

The Meme Machine, created by the collective minds of the University of the Pacific Association for Computing Machinery Club, has the purpose of downloading, rating, and creating memes. We currently only have the code for downloading memes implemented. However, most of the people who began this project have either graduated or dropped the project. Seeing as I wrote a large portion of the downloading code, I add it to my list of projects because I would like to see it finished someday.

Shut In

Shut In is a short film created by a group of high school students in 2012 through the Go Film! workshop held at the San Joaquin County Office of Education in Stockton, CA. It tells the story of a man who lost his father and his job, eventually being confronted by his brother who encourages him to apply at a friend's workplace. I worked as the Gaffer, Foley Artist, and am uncredited as a Sound Editor. Throughout that workshop, all of us also learned, in addition to our individual roles, camera framing techniques, editing in Final Cut Pro (skills I eventually transferred over to Adobe Premiere), writing, and more.

Glove Guitar

This was started by a couple of high schoolers in my Computer Science class. Our idea was to make a guitarless guitar with gloves. Kind of like if you were playing a video game. Anyways, one of my friends and I who were part of the group decided to continue it. We haven't made much progress, but the idea is there.

Proto-Toran et al.

Proto-Toran (and its many daughter languages) are languages that I'm creating for my book and video game that reside in the same universe. I've been told that Proto-Toran sounds a lot like Spanish/Latin and Japanese mixed together. I'll let you be the judge of that.
